## How permissions work

record me uses the browser's `getDisplayMedia` (screen) and `getUserMedia` (camera + microphone) APIs. These require explicit consent — your browser shows a native permission prompt before any capture begins.

## Screen capture

When you choose a screen or window-based mode, your browser displays a picker showing available windows, tabs, and screens. You select what to share. record me can only capture what you explicitly choose — it cannot access other windows silently.

## Camera access

Camera permission is requested when you choose **Screen + Camera + Cursor** or **Camera Only** mode. The browser shows a device picker if you have multiple cameras. Deny camera access and the recording falls back to screen-only (in modes that support it).

## Microphone access

Microphone capture is requested automatically alongside screen and camera permissions. You can deny it and the recording continues silently. The output file will have no audio track.

## Revoking permissions

To revoke permissions after a session, click the lock/camera icon in your browser's address bar and toggle the relevant permission off. The change takes effect immediately on the next recording attempt.

## Private tabs / incognito

Browser permission policies vary in private tabs. In Chrome, previously granted permissions may not carry over to incognito sessions — record me will prompt again.
